BACKGROUND  
In this event, the athletes compete in rowing pairs races. Each race is between boats with two rowers, and the boat that wins is the one that can sustain a higher average boat speed, determined by the rowers’ intrinsic power and technique together with how much effort they put into that race.  
There is also an occasional single-scull race with one rower per boat.  
Whenever athletes row in pairs, they always row with the same partner throughout the event.  
All races take place on the same day.

CONDITIONS  
In the first race, Daniel and Hugo beat Leo and Mark.  
In the second race, Daniel and Hugo beat Owen and Felix.  
In the third race, Leo and Mark beat Daniel and Hugo.  
In the fourth race, Daniel beat Leo.

QUERIES  
Query 1: Out of 100 random rowers, where do you think Daniel ranks in terms of intrinsic rowing strength and technique?  
Query 2: On a percentage scale from 0 to 100%, how much effort do you think Leo put into the third race?  
Query 3: In a new race later this same day between Daniel and Hugo (Boat 1) and Leo and Mark (Boat 2), who would win and by how much?  
Query 4: In a new race later this same day between Owen and Felix (Boat 1) and Leo and Mark (Boat 2), who would win and by how much?
