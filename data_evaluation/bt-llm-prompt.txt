<FLIGHT>

The choice of a flight depends on 4 features:
1. departure time
2. duration
3. number of stops
4. cost

For each feature, the user's preference weight can be one of 5 levels:
1. strongly prefers lower values
2. somewhat prefers lower values
3. indifferent
4. somewhat prefers higher values
5. strongly prefers higher values

<HOTEL>

The choice of a hotel depends on 4 features: distance to downtown, price, rating, and amenities.
Distance and price are numeric features. Rating and amenities are ordinal features.

For each feature, the user's preference weight can be one of 5 levels:
1. strongly prefers lower values
2. somewhat prefers lower values
3. indifferent
4. somewhat prefers higher values
5. strongly prefers higher values

<SHOPPING>

A shopping goal consists of: desired attributes, such as “waterproof” or “soft sole”; desired options, such as “color = black and blue” or “size = XL”; a price limit.

For each feature, the user's preference weight can be one of 5 levels:
1. irrelevant
2. slightly important
3. moderately important
4. very important
5. essential
