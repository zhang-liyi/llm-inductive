Idea: sample from joint distribution (instead of inferring posterior) to construct these input-output pairs. Scenario = Background + Conditions + Queries. ‘Background’ is a description of the sport and team-size. ‘Conditions’ are a few match observations. ‘Queries’ ask about individual strengths, effort-in-match, and some future match result.

For i in range(num_backgrounds):
* Sample the joint distribution, 
* For j in range (num_matches): 
* * Sample variables: individual inherent strengths, which individuals are playing, their effort-in-match, and match result

Caveat: each scenario comes with, say, 3 match observations. So the individual inherent strength variable can be fixed every M matches (M > 3).

For every M matches, the first of these 3 matches form the Conditions and are concatenated with the Background. These are then concatenated with the Queries. These then constitute the Scenario.

How to form the Queries and their ground truth answers?
Query on inherent strength: pick a random individual from this Scenario (the answer is already given; just the already-sampled individual strength).
Query on effort-in-match: pick a random individual from this Scenario (the answer is already given, similarly).
Query on a future match result: since M > 3, we ask what would happen in the match(es) that has already been sampled (those M-3 matches).
