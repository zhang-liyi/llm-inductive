BACKGROUND
In this event, the players are competing in a series of table tennis matches. In each match, the outcome depends on the players’ intrinsic racket control, reflexes, and tactical awareness, along with the effort they put into that particular match.
Matches are played either 1-on-1 or as 4-player team ties.
Most players stick with fixed 4-player teams (Aaron, Ben, Carl, and Devon on one team; Erik, Felix, George, and Henry on another), while Linda joins different lineups.
All matches take place on the same day.

CONDITIONS
In the first team tie, Aaron, Ben, Carl, and Devon beat Erik, Felix, George, and Henry.
In the second team tie, Linda, Erik, Felix, and George lost to Aaron, Ben, Carl, and Devon.
In the third team tie, Linda, Aaron, Ben, and Carl lost to Erik, Felix, George, and Henry.
In the fourth match, Linda lost to Aaron.

QUERIES
Query 1: Out of 100 random table tennis players, where do you think Aaron ranks in terms of intrinsic ability?
Query 2: On a percentage scale from 0 to 100%, how much effort do you think Linda put into the fourth match?
Query 3: In a new 4-player team tie later this same day between Aaron, Ben, Carl, and Devon (Team 1) and Linda, Erik, Felix, and George (Team 2), who would win and by how much?
Query 4: In a new 1-on-1 match later this same day between Linda (Player 1) and Felix (Player 2), who would win and by how much?
