BACKGROUND
In this event, the athletes are competing in an indoor field hockey series. Each team fields 9 players at a time. The result of each match depends on players’ intrinsic stick‑handling, passing, and defensive ability, along with the effort they put in.
Most athletes play on stable teams: the Lynx, the Falcons, and the Vipers. Omar is a rotating player who sometimes fills in for different teams. There are also individual shootout contests between attackers and goalkeepers.
All matches occur on the same day.

CONDITIONS
In the first match, the Lynx with Omar in the lineup lost to the Falcons.
In the second match, the Vipers with Omar in the lineup lost to the Lynx’s regular squad.
In the third match, the Falcons beat the Vipers using their regular squads without Omar.
In an individual shootout contest, Omar lost to Leo from the Falcons.

QUERIES
Query 1: Out of 100 random field hockey players, where do you think Omar ranks in terms of intrinsic field hockey ability?
Query 2: On a percentage scale from 0 to 100%, how much effort do you think Omar put into the first match?
Query 3: In a new match later this same day between the Falcons and the Lynx with Omar added to the Lynx, who would win and by how much?
Query 4: In a new match later this same day between the Falcons and the Vipers with Omar added to the Vipers, who would win and by how much?
