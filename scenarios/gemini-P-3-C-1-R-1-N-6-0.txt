BACKGROUND
In this event, athletes compete in a series of rowing races in "eights" (boats with 8 rowers). The winning boat is determined by the collective speed generated by the crew, which depends on the rowers' intrinsic power and synchronization, modulated by the effort they exert in that specific race.
Athletes generally belong to fixed squads, but two rowers rotate between boats.
All races take place on the same day.

CONDITIONS
In the first race, the crew of Al, Bo, Cy, Di, Ed, Fi, Gy, and Hal beat the crew of Ian, Jo, Ko, Li, Mo, Nu, Oz, and **Zane**.
In the second race, the crew of Ian, Jo, Ko, Li, Mo, Nu, Oz, and Hal beat the crew of Al, Bo, Cy, Di, Ed, Fi, Gy, and **Zane**.
In the third race, the crew of Al, Bo, Cy, Di, Ed, Fi, Gy, and **Zane** beat the crew of Ian, Jo, Ko, Li, Mo, Nu, Oz, and Hal.

QUERIES
Query 1: Out of 100 random athletes, where do you think **Zane** ranks in terms of intrinsic strength?
Query 2: On a percentage scale from 0 to 100%, how much effort do you think Hal put into the third race?
Query 3: In a new race later this same day between the crew of Al, Bo, Cy, Di, Ed, Fi, Gy, and Hal (Team 1) and the crew of Ian, Jo, Ko, Li, Mo, Nu, Oz, and **Zane** (Team 2), who would win and by how much?
Query 4: In a new race later this same day between a crew of Al, Bo, Cy, Ian, Jo, Ko, Li, and **Zane** (Team 1) and a crew of Di, Ed, Fi, Gy, Mo, Nu, Oz, and Hal (Team 2), who would win and by how much?
