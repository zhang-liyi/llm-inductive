BACKGROUND
In this event, players are taking part in a series of baseball contests. In each contest, performance depends on their intrinsic baseball ability (hitting, pitching, fielding) and how much effort and focus they put into that contest.
Players compete either individually (for example, in hitting duels) or in 9‑a‑side games.
There are three club teams—the Eagles, the Bears, and the Sharks—with generally fixed 9‑player lineups. Only one player, Jordan, fills in for different teams in different games during the day.
All contests are played on the same day.

CONDITIONS
In the first contest, a hitting duel, Jordan beat Chris.
In the second game, Jordan and the Eagles beat the Bears.
In the third game, Jordan and the Sharks lost to the Eagles.

QUERIES
Query 1: Out of 100 random baseball players, where do you think Jordan ranks in terms of intrinsic baseball ability?
Query 2: On a percentage scale from 0 to 100%, how much effort do you think Chris put into the first contest?
Query 3: In a new 9‑a‑side game later this same day between Jordan and the Eagles (Team 1) and Chris and the Bears (Team 2), who would win and by how much?
Query 4: In a new 9‑a‑side game later this same day between Jordan and the Sharks (Team 1) and Alex and the Bears (Team 2), who would win and by how much?
