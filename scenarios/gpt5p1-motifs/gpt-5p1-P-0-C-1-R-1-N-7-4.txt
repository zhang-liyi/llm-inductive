BACKGROUND
In this event, the athletes are competing in a series of softball games. Each game’s result depends on the players’ intrinsic softball ability (such as hitting, throwing, and fielding) combined with the level of effort they apply in that game.
Games are played either 1 on 1 batting‑pitching duels or full games between two teams of 9 players.
All games take place on the same day.
Most players remain on their regular team, but Keiko is moved between different teams.

CONDITIONS
In the first game, the Rivercats (Keiko, Adam, Brooke, Carlos, Dana, Ethan, Faith, Gabe, and Holly) beat the Thunderhawks (Ian, Jenna, Kyle, Lana, Mason, Nina, Oscar, Paige, and Ryan).
In the second game, the Silverfoxes (Tara, Victor, Will, Ximena, Yasmin, Zack, Chloe, Drew, and Keiko) beat the Thunderhawks (Ian, Jenna, Kyle, Lana, Mason, Nina, Oscar, Paige, and Ryan).
In the third game, Keiko beat Ian.

QUERIES
Query 1: Out of 100 random softball players, where do you think Keiko ranks in terms of intrinsic softball ability?
Query 2: On a percentage scale from 0 to 100%, how much effort do you think Ian put into the third game?
Query 3: In a new 9‑player‑per‑side game later this same day between Keiko, Adam, Brooke, Carlos, Dana, Ethan, Faith, Gabe, and Holly (Team 1) and Ian, Jenna, Kyle, Lana, Mason, Nina, Oscar, Paige, and Ryan (Team 2), who would win and by how much?
Query 4: In a new 9‑player‑per‑side game later this same day between Tara, Victor, Will, Ximena, Yasmin, Zack, Chloe, Drew, and Nina (Team 1, without Keiko) and Keiko, Adam, Brooke, Carlos, Dana, Ethan, Faith, Gabe, and Oscar (Team 2), who would win and by how much?
