BACKGROUND  
In this table tennis event, players compete in team ties and singles matches. Results depend on their intrinsic racket control, reaction speed, and consistency, together with the effort they put into each match.  
Players compete either in 4-player team ties (with multiple individual rubbers) or in standalone singles matches.  
Most players remain on fixed 4-player teams, but Sonia is reassigned to different lineups during the event.  
All matches take place on the same day.

CONDITIONS  
In the first team tie, Alex, Brian, Colin, and Derek beat Sonia, Helen, Iris, and Kate.  
In the second team tie, Liam, Noah, Owen, and Paul beat Sonia, Kate, Maria, and Nina.  
In a later singles match, Sonia beat Kate.  
In the final team tie, Alex, Brian, Colin, and Derek beat Sonia, Maria, Helen, and Nina.

QUERIES  
Query 1: Out of 100 random table tennis players, where do you think Sonia ranks in terms of intrinsic playing strength?  
Query 2: On a percentage scale from 0 to 100%, how much effort do you think Kate put into the singles match against Sonia?  
Query 3: In a new team tie later this same day between Sonia, Helen, Iris, and Kate (Team 1) and Alex, Brian, Colin, and Derek (Team 2), who would win and by how much?  
Query 4: In a new team tie later this same day between Sonia, Kate, Maria, and Nina (Team 1) and Liam, Noah, Owen, and Paul (Team 2), who would win and by how much?
