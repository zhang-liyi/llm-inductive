BACKGROUND
In this event, the athletes are competing in a series of distance‑running races, including solo time trials and long‑relay team races. In each race, the outcome depends on the runners’ intrinsic aerobic capacity, running economy, mental toughness, and how much effort they put into that race.
Athletes compete either individually or as part of a 9‑runner relay team.
All races take place on the same day. Most runners remain attached to a single 9‑runner squad, but one runner, Lin, is reassigned to different relay teams during the event.

CONDITIONS
In the first relay, Lin, Carla, and seven other Ridgeview runners lost to Felix, Noor, and six other Lakewood runners.
In the second relay, Lin, Felix, and seven other Lakewood runners lost to Jonah, Mia, and six other Brookside runners.
In an evening 5k time‑trial, Lin beat Carla.
In the final relay, Lin, Jonah, and seven other Brookside runners lost to Noor, Mia, and seven other Lakewood runners.

QUERIES
Query 1: Out of 100 random distance runners, where do you think Lin ranks in terms of intrinsic distance‑running ability?
Query 2: On a percentage scale from 0 to 100%, how much effort do you think Felix put into the second relay?
Query 3: In a new relay race later this same day between Lin, Carla, and seven other Ridgeview runners (Team 1) and Felix, Noor, and seven other Lakewood runners (Team 2), who would win and by how much?
Query 4: In a new relay race later this same day between Lin, Jonah, and seven other Brookside runners (Team 1) and Mia, Noor, and seven other Lakewood runners (Team 2), who would win and by how much?
