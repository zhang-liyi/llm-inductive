BACKGROUND
In this competition, athletes play Lacrosse (Sixes variant adapted to 7 players). The winner is decided by the collective stick skills and agility of the team, based on intrinsic coordination and the effort exerted during the game.
There are 7 players per team.
All games are played consecutively.

CONDITIONS
In the first game, the team of Ace, Bud, Cal, Don, Ed, Fin, and Gus beat the team of Ivan, Hal, Ian, Jay, Ken, Lou, and Moe.
In the second game, the team of Hal, Ian, Jay, Ken, Lou, Moe, and Gus beat the team of Ivan, Ace, Bud, Cal, Don, Ed, and Fin.
In the third game, the team of Ace, Hal, Bud, Ian, Cal, Jay, and Don lost to the team of Ivan, Ed, Ken, Fin, Lou, Gus, and Moe.

QUERIES
Query 1: Out of 100 random athletes, where do you think Ivan ranks in terms of intrinsic coordination?
Query 2: On a percentage scale from 0 to 100%, how much effort do you think Gus put into the second game?
Query 3: In a new game later this same day between the team of Ace, Bud, Cal, Don, Ed, Fin, and Hal (Team 1) and the team of Ivan, Ian, Jay, Ken, Lou, Moe, and Gus (Team 2), who would win and by how much?
Query 4: In a new game later this same day between the team of Ivan, Ace, Hal, Bud, Ian, Cal, and Jay (Team 1) and the team of Don, Ed, Ken, Fin, Lou, Gus, and Moe (Team 2), who would win and by how much?
